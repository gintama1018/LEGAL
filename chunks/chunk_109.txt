(2) Nothing in this article shall— (a) be deemed to transfer to the Governor any functions conferred by any existing law on any other authority; or (b) prevent Parliament or the Legislature of the State from conferring by law functions on any authority subordinate to the Governor. 155. Appointment of Governor.—The Governor of a State shall be appointed by the President by warrant under his hand and seal. 156. Term of office of Governor.—(1) The Governor shall hold office during the pleasure of the President. (2) The Governor may, by writing under his hand addressed to the President, resign his office.
